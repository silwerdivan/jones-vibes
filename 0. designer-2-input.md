Here is my Lead Design pitch for **"The Math of Life, Cyberpunk Edition,"** breaking down how we’ll use the MDA Framework (Mechanics, Dynamics, Aesthetics) to turn a browser-based spreadsheet into an addictive, satirical masterpiece.

---

### 1. The Core Philosophy (MDA Framework)

*   **Mechanics (The Rules):** A strict allocation of 168 Hours per week, divided among Jobs, Education, Recovery, and Hustles. Actions manipulate four core meters: **Credits (₡), Energy (⚡), Sanity (🧠), and Health (❤️).**
*   **Dynamics (The Play):** The "Poverty Trap." Early game dynamics force players to borrow against their future (e.g., taking stimulants to work a double shift, costing Health and Sanity tomorrow). Mid-game shifts to optimization. Late-game is about automation and dominance.
*   **Aesthetics (The Feeling):** Oppression giving way to ruthless empowerment. We want the player to start feeling the crushing weight of gig-economy capitalism, but eventually experience the god-complex of a Corporate Executive. 

### 2. Core Features & Systems

To make a DOM-based UI game deeply engaging, we rely on Bartle’s taxonomy, specifically targeting **Achievers** (optimizing the math) and **Explorers** (discovering narrative paths and rare tech).

*   **The "Subscription" Economy (Rent & Upkeep):** 
    Instead of just "Rent," the player has a fixed weekly "Burn Rate." Rent, Oxygen Tax, Synthetic Protein Subscriptions, and Cyberware Maintenance. The core tension is beating your Burn Rate before Friday night.
*   **The Cyberware System (Talent Tree):**
    Players can visit the "Ripperdoc Modal" to install upgrades. *Example: "Optic HUD (Costs 5,000₡). Passive: Reduces Time taken to study by 10%."* But cyberware increases your weekly Burn Rate (maintenance).
*   **The Gig-Board (Quests/Jobs):**
    A swipe-able or scrollable list of jobs. Early game: *“Scrap metal in the Acid Wastes”* (High Energy, Low ₡, Low Sanity). Mid game: *“Corp-Sec Data Entry”* (Low Energy, Medium ₡, Massive Sanity Drain). 
*   **Random Encounters (The Event Bus):**
    Since we are event-driven, every action rolls a hidden D20. Work too much? Trigger the *"Audit"* modal. Walk in the slums? Trigger the *"Mugged by Synth-Punks"* modal. Players can choose how to react based on their stats.
*   **The "Burnout" Mechanic:**
    If Sanity hits 0, the player doesn't die; they experience a "Forced Reboot." They lose 3 days of time, are charged a massive medical bill, and wake up with a "Trauma" debuff.

### 3. The Gameplay Loop: Week-by-Week Run-Down

Let's look at how the game evolves. The beauty of this design is that the UI stays the same, but the *context* and *scale* of the math drastically shift.

#### **Week 1: The Rat Race (Survival Phase)**
*   **Assess:** You have 100₡. Your weekly Burn Rate for your trash-compactor apartment is 150₡. You have full Energy but are one bad day away from eviction.
*   **Plan:** You open the **Gig-Board Modal**. You need fast cash. You allocate 40 Hours to "Sewer Maintenance" (High Energy, Low Sanity). You allocate 20 Hours to "Resting on a broken mattress." You have 10 hours left—you use it to "Study Basic Code" on a cracked datapad.
*   **Execute:** You hit "End Week." 
*   **The Result:** The progress bar zooms across the screen. 
    *   *Event Popup!* "You found a pristine rat carcass. Sell it for 10₡ or eat it for +5 Energy?" You sell it. 
    *   You make rent, but your Sanity is at 15%. The screen pulses with a subtle red CSS vignette. You survived, but you can't do this forever.

#### **Week 15: The Hustle (Optimization Phase)**
*   **Assess:** You’ve saved enough to move out of the slums. You are now a Level 2 "Code-Monkey." Your Burn Rate is higher (500₡) because you bought a *Neural-Link* to study faster.
*   **Plan:** You no longer do manual labor. You allocate 40 hours to your Corporate Job. But wait! The Black Market tab is glowing. A hacker offers you a side-gig: "Launder Crypto for the Yakuza." It takes 10 Hours, pays 1,000₡, but carries a 20% risk of "Arrest." 
*   **Execute:** You take the risk. You allocate your remaining time to the VR-Club to restore your Sanity. Hit "End Week."
*   **The Result:** 
    *   *Event Popup!* "Corporate Audit." You pass the skill check because of your Neural-Link. 
    *   The Yakuza job succeeds! You now have a massive surplus of cash. You open the Ripperdoc menu and buy a "Sub-Dermal Adrenaline Pump" (Increases Max Energy by 20%). You are clawing your way up.

#### **Week 52: The Apex (Dominance Phase)**
*   **Assess:** You are a Corpo-Exec. Your Burn Rate is 50,000₡ a week (Penthouse, Luxury Organs, VIP Security). 
*   **Plan:** You don't work "Gigs" anymore. You manage *Investments* and *Corporate Sabotage*. You allocate time to "Hostile Takeover of Rival Tech Firm." You allocate Energy to "Schmooze with Board Members." 
*   **Execute:** Hit "End Week."
*   **The Result:** Your automated investments pay your rent. The challenge now isn't money; it's surviving assassination attempts (Health drain) and managing the sheer stress (Sanity drain) of being at the top. You have beaten the math of life; you *are* the math now.
